# 💧 Water Cycle — Model & Explain

Understand and model how water moves through the environment (evaporation, condensation, precipitation, collection). Connect each stage to real‑world examples and weather.

## Learning Goals
- Name and describe the main stages of the water cycle.
- Explain how the Sun drives the cycle and why it matters for weather and life.
- Build a simple model or diagram and explain each part.

## Read & Explore (10–15 min)
1) Skim a kid‑friendly reference of your choice (textbook, library book, or a short video) to review the four core stages.
2) Think of a local example for each stage (e.g., puddles drying = evaporation).

## Activities
Choose the track for your grade.

### Elementary Grade
- Draw the water cycle with arrows for evaporation, condensation, precipitation, and collection.
- Add 1 real‑life example label for each stage (e.g., “clouds over the mountains”).
- Write 3–4 sentences explaining how the Sun powers the cycle.

### Middle School Grade
- Create a labeled diagram OR a one‑page summary that includes: evaporation, transpiration, condensation, precipitation, runoff, infiltration/groundwater.
- Add a short paragraph (4–6 sentences) explaining how temperature, wind, and landscape affect the cycle in your region.
- Include one human‑impact example (e.g., urban runoff) and its effect.
