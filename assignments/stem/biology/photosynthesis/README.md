# ☀️ Photosynthesis — Energy from Light

Explore how plants use sunlight to make sugar, releasing oxygen into the air.

## Learning Goals
- State the simple photosynthesis idea in words and the core balanced equation (7th grade).
- Explain why sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide are necessary.
- Connect photosynthesis to food chains and oxygen.

## Read & Explore (10–15 min)
Find a kid‑friendly explanation of photosynthesis. Focus on: sunlight, chlorophyll/chloroplasts, water (H₂O), carbon dioxide (CO₂), sugar (glucose), oxygen (O₂).

## Activities
Choose the track for your grade.

### Elementary Grade
- Draw a plant with arrows for Sun → leaf, CO₂ in, O₂ out, water from roots.
- Write 3–4 sentences: What goes in? What comes out? Why do we care?

### Middle School Grade
- Write the photosynthesis equation: 6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ (in words is fine if you can’t format).
- In 5–7 sentences, explain: role of chlorophyll, where it happens (chloroplasts), and how photosynthesis links to cellular respiration.
