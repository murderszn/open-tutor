# 🧪 Reproduction — How Living Things Make More Life

Compare asexual and sexual reproduction and introduce cell division (mitosis vs meiosis) at the right depth for each grade.

## Learning Goals
- Define asexual vs sexual reproduction with simple examples.
- Distinguish mitosis (growth/repair, identical cells) from meiosis (gametes, half the chromosomes) at a high level.
- Explain why genetic variation matters.

## Read & Explore (10–15 min)
Use a kid‑friendly source to review: asexual (budding, binary fission, runners) vs sexual (joining of egg and sperm). For 7th grade, add mitosis vs meiosis basics.

## Activities
Choose the track for your grade.

### Elementary Grade
- Make a T‑chart with two columns: Asexual vs Sexual. Add 2 examples in each column and 1 sentence to describe each.
- In 2–3 sentences, explain why some species use one strategy or the other.

### Middle School Grade
- Write a short comparison (6–8 sentences) of mitosis vs meiosis: purpose, number of divisions, chromosome number, and whether the cells are identical or varied.
- Give a real example of asexual reproduction and one of sexual reproduction; explain a benefit of genetic variation.
