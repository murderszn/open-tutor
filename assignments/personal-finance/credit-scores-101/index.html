💳 Credit Scores 101

What a credit score measures, how it’s built, and how it changes APR and access to credit for housing, cars, business loans, and personal credit cards/loans.

What is a Credit Score?

A credit score is a 3‑digit number (typically 300–850) that summarizes your history with borrowing and paying back money. Lenders use it to guess how risky it is to lend to you.

What factors matter?

  • Payment history (~35%): On‑time payments vs. late/missed.
  • Amounts owed / utilization (~30%): Balance ÷ limit. Under ~30% is healthier.
  • Length of history (~15%): How long accounts have been open.
  • New credit (~10%): Recent hard inquiries / new accounts.
  • Credit mix (~10%): Different types (installment vs. revolving).

    What this means

    Higher scores generally get lower APRs, leading to lower monthly payments and much less total interest over time. Lower scores may face higher APRs and more denials or lower credit limits.

    Credit Models & Bureaus

    FICO Score is the most widely used credit score. It ranges from 300–850 and has different versions (and some industry‑specific versions) that lenders may choose from. Another model you might see is VantageScore. It’s normal for scores to differ a bit across models and over time.

    How they get data: Most banks, credit card companies, auto lenders, and other creditors choose to report your account status to one or more bureaus every month (balance, limit, on‑time/late payments). Bureaus compile this into your credit file, and scoring models use that file to calculate a score. Not every creditor reports to every bureau, so your three reports may not be identical.

    Tip: Paying on time and keeping balances low are two habits that help across all models and bureaus.

    Credit Cards: Revolving Interest

    Credit cards are revolving accounts. If you don’t pay in full, interest accrues on the remaining balance. Carrying balances month‑to‑month—especially at high APR—gets expensive fast.

    Build and Protect Your Score

    • Pay on time, every time. Set reminders or auto‑pay.
    • Keep utilization low (aim under ~30%; lower is better).
    • Let older accounts age; avoid frequent new accounts.
    • Use a mix you can manage (installment + revolving).
    • Check statements and dispute errors promptly.

    Where Scores Matter

    • 🏠 Housing: Mortgage approvals and rates; some rentals screen credit.
    • 🚗 Cars: Auto loan approvals and APR; lower scores raise monthly payments.
    • 🏢 Business loans: Personal credit can influence small‑business financing, especially early on.
    • 💳 Personal credit: Cards and personal loans vary widely by score band and history.
