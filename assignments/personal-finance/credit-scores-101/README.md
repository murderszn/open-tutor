# 💳 Credit Scores 101 — APR, Interest, and Real‑Life Impact

Learn how credit scores work, what affects them, and how they change the interest you pay on common loans (housing, cars, business, and personal credit cards/loans).

Open the companion mini‑site: [index.html](./index.html)
Core reference: [Financial Tools & Principles](../../../resources/financial_tools_and_principles.md)

## Why This Matters

Your credit score is a quick “trust score” for lenders. Higher scores usually mean lower interest (APR), better loan terms, and more approvals; lower scores mean higher APR, fewer approvals, and smaller credit limits.

## Learning Objectives

- Identify the five major credit score factors and practical habits that improve each.
- Explain how APR changes the total cost of borrowing.
- Compare monthly payment and total interest across different APRs for the same loan.
- Describe how credit score affects access to housing, cars, business loans, and personal credit.

## Tips & Notes

- APR examples are estimates; real offers vary by lender, state, income, and down payment.
- Paying on time and keeping utilization below ~30% are the two biggest wins.
- Avoid opening many new accounts in a short period.
- See also: [Financial Tools & Principles](../../../resources/financial_tools_and_principles.md) and [Ultimate Guide to File Types](../../../resources/file-types.md) if exporting to PDF.
