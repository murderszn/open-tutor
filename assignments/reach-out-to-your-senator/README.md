# 🇺🇸 Assignment: Reach Out to Your Senator (Illinois)

Speak up as an informed, respectful constituent. You will contact one or both current U.S. Senators from Illinois to either express appreciation or share a constructive concern (grievance) about an issue that matters to you.

---

## 🎯 Objective
- Learn how to engage your elected officials respectfully and clearly.
- Practice writing a concise, persuasive message with facts and a specific ask.
- Understand where to find official contact information and issue pages.

---

## 🧭 What You’ll Do (Step‑by‑Step)
1) Choose your purpose
- Appreciation (thank them for a vote, a bill, or something you value), or
- Grievance (politely explain a problem and what you want them to do).

2) Pick one issue
- Examples: education, technology, the environment, healthcare, military families, small business, agriculture, transportation.
- Look up the Senator’s positions or recent statements on that issue (links below).

3) Draft your message
- Use `email-template.md` in this folder.
- Keep it one page or less. Be specific; include one clear “ask” or a clear “thanks.”
- Identify yourself as a constituent (city/ZIP is enough—never share sensitive data).

4) Parent/Guardian review
- Get an adult to review and approve your message before sending.

5) Send it
- Use the Senator’s official contact form (links below). Copy/paste your message. Save a screenshot of the confirmation page.
- Optional: Call one office using the `phone-script-template.md` to practice a short, respectful script.

6) Submit your work
- Save your final message (and optional call notes) to your student folder.
- File name suggestion: `YYYY-MM-DD_senator-outreach.md`.

## 🧠 Tips for a Strong Message
- Be respectful and specific: “I’m a constituent from [City, ZIP]. I appreciate/concerned about …”
- One clear point: appreciation or a single, focused ask.
- Use 1–2 relevant facts or a short personal story.
- Close with thanks and your first name (parents may add last name if they choose).

Kid‑Friendly (Elijah):
- Say thank you or explain your problem in 3–5 short sentences.
- Tell where you live (city/ZIP). Be polite. Ask for one thing.

---

## 🔒 Safety & Privacy
- Never share sensitive personal information (full birthdate, school, IDs).
- Use a parent/guardian email or CC them.
- Parents should review before sending; calls should be supervised.
