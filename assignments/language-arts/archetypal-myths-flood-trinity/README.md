# 📜 Archetypal Myths: Flood Stories & Trinity Patterns

**Grade Level:** Middle School (Grade 7–8)
**Subject:** Language Arts + Social Studies (Comparative Myth & Culture)

## 🎯 Objective / What You Will Learn
* Recognize the “Flood” story as a recurring human archetype across cultures.
* Understand the idea of triads/Trinity patterns in stories and symbols.
* Compare themes to weekly Bible teaching respectfully and thoughtfully.

---

## 🔍 Examples to Explore
Flood Archetype (pick 2–3 to compare):
* Hebrew Bible: Noah’s Ark (Genesis)
* Mesopotamia: Epic of Gilgamesh (Utnapishtim’s flood)
* Other world traditions (e.g., Greek, Hindu, Indigenous flood accounts)

Triads/Trinity Patterns (choose 1–2 patterns):
* Christian Trinity (Father, Son, Holy Spirit)
* Story triads (past/present/future; mind/body/spirit; creator/preserver/destroyer)

---

## 🚀 The Project Steps
1. Read/Watch Summaries: Get short, reputable summaries of at least two flood accounts and one triad concept.
2. Venn Diagram (Flood): Similarities/differences in cause, hero, boat/ark, outcome, moral.
3. Symbol Map (Triads): Draw or list how your chosen triad shows up in stories/symbols today.
4. Relevance Reflection: Use the standard template to connect one flood or triad pattern to modern culture.

---

## 📦 Deliverables
* One Venn diagram comparing two flood stories (photo/PDF acceptable).
* One short symbol map of a triad pattern.
* One completed relevance reflection.

---

* [ ] Identifies clear common elements across flood myths.
