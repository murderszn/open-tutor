# ✍🏾 Poetry Focus: Langston Hughes & Maya Angelou

**Grade Level:** Middle School (Grade 7–8)
**Subject:** Language Arts (Poetry & Culture)

## 🎯 Objective / What You Will Learn
* Read and annotate two short poems (at least one by Langston Hughes, one by Maya Angelou).
* Identify voice, imagery, and theme.
* Explain each poem’s cultural impact and modern relevance.

---

## 📜 Suggested Poems
Pick 2–3 total (at least one from each poet):
* Langston Hughes: “Harlem (A Dream Deferred)”, “I, Too”, “Mother to Son”.
* Maya Angelou: “Still I Rise”, “Phenomenal Woman”.

Use a printed copy or trusted online text. Hearing a recitation can help.

---

## 🚀 The Project Steps
1. Read Aloud: Read each poem twice (once silent, once aloud).
2. Annotate: Circle 3 powerful words/lines per poem; in the margin, note why they hit you.
3. Theme & Voice: In 4–5 sentences, explain the poem’s message and tone.
4. Relevance Reflection: Use the standard template for ONE poem; connect to pop culture, music, or current events.

---

## 📦 Deliverables
* Annotated poem pages (photo/PDF) or typed notes.
* A short paragraph per poem on theme & voice.
* One completed relevance reflection using the shared template.

---

## 📋 [For Parents] Evaluation Checklist
* [ ] Clear annotations (3+ lines/words marked per poem) with reasons.
* [ ] Theme/tone explained in the student’s own words.
* [ ] Relevance reflection provides at least 2 modern connections.
* [ ] Writing is neat, specific, and proofread.
