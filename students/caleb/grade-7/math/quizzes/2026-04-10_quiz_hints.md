# 🧠 Hints for April 10, 2026 — Rational Numbers (Negatives)

Socratic reminders, not answers. Track signs carefully and write intermediate steps.

---

## Subtracting Integers = Adding the Opposite
- Rewrite `a − b` as `a + (−b)`.
- Example form to copy (not your numbers): `−8 − (−10)` → `−8 + 10`.

## Number Line Intuition
- Adding a positive moves right; adding a negative moves left.
- Distance is the magnitude; direction is the sign.

## Combining Signs Quickly
- Same sign → add magnitudes, keep the sign.
- Opposite signs → subtract magnitudes, keep the sign of the larger magnitude.

## Pitfalls to Avoid
- Don’t drop parentheses: the minus sign in front of parentheses applies to the entire quantity.
- Double negatives: `−(−x)` becomes `+x`.

## Self‑Check
1) Did I convert subtraction to addition of the opposite when helpful?
2) Did I decide the final sign based on magnitude, not position in the expression?
3) Does a quick number‑line sketch agree with my result’s sign?
