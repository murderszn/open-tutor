# 🧮 7th Grade Weekly Math Quizzes

This document contains 12 weekly math quizzes designed to test standard 7th-grade math concepts throughout the curriculum. 

Parents: Have Caleb complete one quiz every Friday. Use the `assessment_grader.md` AI persona to grade these if you need assistance!

---

## Week 1: Ratios & Rates
1. A recipe calls for 2 cups of flour to make 12 cookies. How many cups of flour are needed to make 30 cookies?
2. A car travels 180 miles in 3 hours. What is the car's speed in miles per hour (mph)?
3. If 5 apples cost $3.50, what is the unit price (cost per apple)?

## Week 2: Proportional Relationships
1. Is the relationship between $x$ and $y$ proportional if the points $(2, 4)$, $(3, 6)$, and $(5, 12)$ are on the line? Why or why not?
2. A map has a scale of 1 inch = 50 miles. If two cities are 4.5 inches apart on the map, how far apart are they in real life?
3. Solve for $x$: $3/4 = x/20$

## Week 3: Operations with Fractions
1. Calculate: $3/4 + 1/3$
2. Calculate: $2 \ 1/2 \times 3/5$
3. A piece of wood is $8 \ 3/4$ inches long. If you cut off a piece that is $2 \ 1/8$ inches long, how much wood is left?

## Week 4: Rational Numbers (Negatives)
1. Calculate: $-15 + 8$
2. Calculate: $-4 \times -6$
3. If the temperature drops from $5^\circ\text{F}$ to $-12^\circ\text{F}$, what is the total change in temperature?

## Week 5: Equivalent Expressions
1. Simplify: $3(2x + 4) - 2x$
2. Factor the expression: $12x + 18$
3. Are $4(x - 2)$ and $4x - 8$ equivalent expressions? Show your work.

## Week 6: Solving Equations
1. Solve for $x$: $2x + 7 = 15$
2. Solve for $y$: $(y/3) - 4 = 2$
3. A gym charges a $\$20$ membership fee plus $\$10$ per month. If you spent $\$140$ total, how many months were you a member? Set up an equation and solve.

## Week 7: Solving Inequalities
1. Solve and graph the inequality: $3x - 5 > 10$
2. Solve: $-2x \le 8$
3. You have $\$50$ to spend at the fair. Admission is $\$10$, and each ride costs $\$3$. Write an inequality to find the maximum number of rides ($r$) you can go on.

## Week 8: Geometry - Angles
1. Two angles are supplementary. If one angle measures $45^\circ$, what is the measure of the other angle?
2. Two angles are complementary. If one angle is $2x$ and the other is $3x$, solve for $x$.
3. What is the sum of the interior angles of a triangle?

## Week 9: Geometry - Area & Circumference
1. What is the area of a circle with a radius of 5 cm? (Use $\pi \approx 3.14$)
2. What is the circumference of a circle with a diameter of 10 inches?
3. A rectangular garden is 12 feet long and 8 feet wide. What is its area?

## Week 10: Geometry - Volume & Surface Area
1. What is the volume of a rectangular prism with length = 4, width = 3, and height = 5?
2. What is the surface area of a cube with side length = 3?
3. If the volume of a box is 60 cubic inches, and the base area is 15 square inches, what is the height?

## Week 11: Statistics & Sampling
1. You want to know the favorite lunch food of 7th graders at your school. Is asking only the students at your lunch table a random sample? Why or why not?
2. The test scores for 5 students are 85, 90, 92, 88, and 95. What is the mean (average) score?
3. What is the median of the following data set: 12, 15, 11, 20, 18?

## Week 12: Probability
1. If you roll a standard 6-sided die, what is the probability of rolling a number greater than 4?
2. You flip a coin and roll a 6-sided die. What is the probability of getting "Heads" and a "3"?
3. A bag contains 3 red marbles, 2 blue marbles, and 5 green marbles. What is the probability of pulling out a red marble?
