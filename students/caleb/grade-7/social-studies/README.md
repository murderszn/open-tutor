# 🌍 7th Grade Social Studies

Welcome to your Social Studies folder! Save your history notes, geography assignments, and projects here.

## Curriculum Goals (End of Year Expectations)

### 1. Geography
*   **The United States**: Know all 50 US states and their locations.
*   **Global Geography**: 
    *   Name and locate all oceans and continents.
    *   Align major countries to their respective continents.
    *   Understand the general language and religious profiles of different regions around the world.

### 2. Civics & Government
*   **Parts of the Government**: Understand the three branches of the US government (Executive, Legislative, Judicial) and their functions.
*   **Government Officials**: Be able to categorize government-paid officials into their correct part of the government.
*   **Local & State Leaders**: Know the names and roles of your local Mayor and state Governor.

## Integrated Units (use our shared resources)

Use these rotating mini‑units to connect geography, data, and measurement. Submit short write‑ups or screenshots for each.

### 1) Global Trade & Chokepoints
- Read: [World Facts](../../../../resources/world_facts.md) → Geography & Trade Routes.
- Task: Open Google Maps links for the Panama Canal, Suez Canal, Strait of Hormuz, and Strait of Malacca. For each, list two nearby countries and one export commonly shipped through it.
- Measure: Using [Weights & Measures](../../../../resources/weights_and_measures.md), convert 5,000 miles to kilometers and 10,000 km to miles. Explain which units are used in shipping and why.

### 2) Countries & Data Literacy
- Open: [UN Countries (CSV)](../../../../resources/un_countries.csv).
- Task: Pick ONE region (e.g., “South America”). List the 5 most populous countries in that region with their capitals. Include Google Maps links (from the CSV or by searching).
- Bonus: Note one language family or major language for the region and one interesting demographic fact (population density, area, or urbanization) using the CSV fields.

### 3) Capitals & Economic Hubs
- Read: World Facts → Capitals & Economic Hubs. Click each capital’s Google Maps link.
- Task: For three capitals on different continents, write 2–3 sentences on why each is economically important (port access, river, population, finance center, etc.).

### 4) Food, Measures, and Trade
- Read: World Facts → Food Commodities — Leaders (wheat/soy/rice).
- Task: Choose one commodity. Identify the top three producers and sketch the likely shipping route to the U.S. or Europe via a chokepoint.
- Measure: Convert 1 million metric tons to kilograms and to pounds using Weights & Measures. Show your math.

## Weekly Rotation (repeat every 4 weeks)
- Week A: Global Trade & Chokepoints
- Week B: Countries & Data Literacy
- Week C: Capitals & Economic Hubs
- Week D: Food, Measures, and Trade

Use Friday to summarize what you learned (5–7 sentences) and file it here.
