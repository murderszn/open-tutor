## Daily Requirement
* **Read or watch a summary for at least 30 minutes daily.**
  - Acceptable inputs: book chapters, reputable summaries, teacher videos, or film scenes (parent‑approved).
  - Output: a short note, outline, or a filled **Relevance Reflection** for major works.

## Curriculum Goals

### 1. Grammar & Mechanics
* Master the **basic parts of a sentence** (subject, predicate, nouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs).
* Progress into complex sentence structures, clauses, and phrases.

### 2. Historical & Classical Literature (Archetypes)
* **Ancient Epics & Texts**: The Bible, The Epic of Gilgamesh, Homer’s epics.
* **Shakespeare Overviews**: Understand plots/themes of major plays without full reenactment.
* **1500s–1800s Classics**: High‑level selections to see how archetypes evolve (e.g., The Scarlet Letter).

### 3. Modern Literature & Culture
* Discuss **culturally relevant books/poems** and explain their impact on society and pop culture.
* Cross‑link with Social Studies eras when useful.

## Weekly Video Review (Classic Books)

Each week, pick one high‑quality YouTube summary or analysis for a classic work. Watch, then write a short reflection (5–8 sentences):
- What is the core conflict/theme?
- One archetype you noticed and where it appears.
- One modern connection (pop culture, news, or your life).
