# 📐 Golden Ratio — Elijah (4th Grade)

Assignment spec: ../../../../../assignments/math/golden-ratio/README.md

What to do
- Watch 1 short video about the golden ratio or Fibonacci spirals.
- Draw a big rectangle on paper. Make its sides about 16 cm by 10 cm (close to 1.6:1). Inside, draw squares that fit, then a curving spiral line through them.
- Look around home or in a photo for a rectangle (screen, book, picture). Measure length and width in centimeters; divide L ÷ W. Is it close to 1.6?
- Bonus: Make a simple poster (title + picture) and try placing parts using the bigger side vs smaller side.

- Optional: Watch the fullscreen Splitter animate the ratio: ../../../../../assignments/math/golden-ratio/resources/splitter/index.html (ask a parent for help)

Deliverable
- Half page: one drawing, one measured example with the L ÷ W number, and 2–3 sentences on what you noticed.

Helpful resources
- Golden Ratio spec (above)
- Wikipedia — Golden ratio (with an adult): https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Golden_ratio
- Optional interactive (with an adult): ../../../../../assignments/math/golden-ratio/resources/golden-rectangle/index.html

Rubric
- Try your best on measuring (neat numbers), clear drawing, and a short explanation.
