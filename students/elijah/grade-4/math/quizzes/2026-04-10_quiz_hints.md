# 🧠 Hints for April 10, 2026 — Place Value & Rounding

These are gentle nudges to help you think it through. No answers here — you’ve got this!

---

## Place Value Check
- Look at the digit that decides rounding: for hundreds, peek at the tens place; for tens, peek at the ones place.
- If that deciding digit is 0–4, the number “stays”; if it’s 5–9, the number “jumps up.”
- Ask yourself: “Which two hundreds is this number between? Which one is it closer to?”

## Rounding to the Nearest Hundred
- Circle the hundreds digit. Put a box around the tens digit. The boxed digit decides what the circled one does.
- Example style to copy (not your exact numbers): 3,6[7]2 → The 7 decides that 36 hundred becomes 37 hundred.

## Rounding to the Nearest Ten
- Circle the tens digit. Box the ones digit. Apply the same 0–4 stays, 5–9 jumps rule.
- Quick check: After you round to tens, the ones digit must always be 0.

## Self‑Check Questions
1) Did I only change the digit I’m rounding to (and set all lower places to zero)?
2) Did I think about a number line between the two closest tens/hundreds?
3) Does my rounded number make sense if I estimate in my head?

Cheer: Every round is a tiny decision — and you’re the detective finding the best fit! 🔍
