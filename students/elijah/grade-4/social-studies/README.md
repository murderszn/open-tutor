# 🌍 4th Grade Social Studies

Welcome to your Social Studies folder! Save your history notes, geography assignments, and projects here.

## Curriculum Goals (End of Year Expectations)

### 1. Geography
*   **The United States**: Know all 50 US states and their locations on a map.
*   **Global Geography**: 
    *   Name and locate all oceans and continents.
    *   Align major countries to their respective continents.
    *   Learn about the general language and religious profiles of different regions around the world.

### 2. Civics & Government
*   **Parts of the Government**: Understand the three branches of the US government (Executive, Legislative, Judicial) and what they basically do.
*   **Government Officials**: Be able to categorize government-paid officials into their correct part of the government (e.g., police, judges, president, senators).
*   **Local & State Leaders**: Know the names and roles of your local Mayor and state Governor.

## Fun Weekly Themes (using our shared resources)

These rotate so you learn a little geography, maps, and measurement every month. Keep it simple: draw, label, and write short answers.

### 1) Map Hunt: Oceans, Seas, and Canals
- Open: [World Facts](../../../../resources/world_facts.md) → Geography & Trade Routes.
- Click the Google Maps links for the Panama Canal, Suez Canal, and Singapore (near the Strait of Malacca).
- Draw one small map with arrows and label two countries each route connects.

### 2) Country Trading Cards
- Open: [UN Countries (CSV)](../../../../resources/un_countries.csv).
- Pick 3 countries (each from a different continent). For each card: write the country name, its capital, and paste the Google Maps link. Draw the flag if you like!

### 3) Capitals & Landmarks
- Read: World Facts → Capitals & Economic Hubs. Click two capitals and zoom in on the map.
- Write 1–2 sentences each: What big river, ocean, or landmark is nearby?

### 4) Weights & Measures on the Map
- Open: [Weights & Measures](../../../../resources/weights_and_measures.md).
- Convert 100 miles to kilometers and 200 kilometers to miles (use the cheat sheet). Then write one sentence: Which unit do most countries use?
