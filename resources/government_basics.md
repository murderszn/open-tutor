## 🌿 The Three Branches — Overview

**Legislative** (makes laws) · **Executive** (carries out laws) · **Judicial** (interprets laws)

Kid‑Friendly:
- Legislative = writes laws (Congress).
- Executive = runs the country day‑to‑day (President + departments).
- Judicial = decides what laws mean (courts).

---

## 📝 Legislative Branch (Congress)

What it does
- Writes and votes on laws. Approves budgets. Confirms many presidential appointments (Senate). Can declare war.

Who’s in it
- House of Representatives (by population) and Senate (2 per state).

Key Legislative Branch agencies (with links)
- **GAO — Government Accountability Office**: audits/reviews for Congress — [gao.gov](https://www.gao.gov/)
- **CBO — Congressional Budget Office**: budget/economic analysis — [cbo.gov](https://www.cbo.gov/)
- **Library of Congress**: research, archives, copyright — [loc.gov](https://www.loc.gov/)
- **CRS — Congressional Research Service** (within LOC): nonpartisan policy research — [crsreports.congress.gov](https://crsreports.congress.gov/)

Learn more
- Congress: [congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/)
- How a Bill Becomes a Law (Congress): [congress.gov/legislative-process](https://www.congress.gov/legislative-process)

Kid‑Friendly:
- The House and Senate write and vote on laws. Helpers (like GAO and CBO) do research and check money math.

## ⚖️ Judicial Branch (Courts)

What it does
- Interprets the law, ensures laws follow the Constitution, resolves disputes.

Court structure (simplified)
- U.S. Supreme Court (highest)
- U.S. Courts of Appeals (circuits)
- U.S. District Courts (trial courts)
- Specialized courts (e.g., tax, claims, bankruptcy)

Key links
- Supreme Court: [supremecourt.gov](https://www.supremecourt.gov/)
- U.S. Courts (maps, basics): [uscourts.gov](https://www.uscourts.gov/)

Kid‑Friendly:
- Judges explain what laws mean and whether rules are fair under the Constitution.

## 🧩 How a Bill Becomes a Law (Quick)

Idea → Bill introduced → Committee work → Floor debate/vote → Other chamber → Conference → Final votes → President (sign or veto) → Law (then implemented by agencies)

Learn more: [congress.gov/legislative-process](https://www.congress.gov/legislative-process)

Kid‑Friendly: A rule is proposed, discussed, changed, and voted on by both House and Senate, then sent to the President.
