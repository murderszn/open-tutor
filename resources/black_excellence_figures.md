# Black Excellence: Study Them

A research guide highlighting historic and modern Black excellence across various industries and domains. This guide introduces students to figures who have broken barriers, set new records, and culturally defined their generations.

## 🎭 The Screen Icons & Entertainers
*   [**Michael B. Jordan**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_B._Jordan): Known for his compelling roles in the *Creed* franchise and *Black Panther*, he is a major leading man and accomplished director. He has also been a powerful advocate for inclusion riders in Hollywood.
*   [**Denzel Washington**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Denzel_Washington): A titan of American cinema, he is a two-time Academy Award winner known for transformative roles in *Malcolm X*, *Training Day*, and *Fences*. He continues to set the gold standard in prestige drama and stage acting.
*   [**Zendaya**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zendaya): Transitioning from a child star to an Emmy-winning powerhouse, she is a dominating force in television (*Euphoria*) and blockbuster films (*Spider-Man*, *Dune*). She is also highly influential in global fashion.
*   [**Viola Davis**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viola_Davis): The sole Black actor to achieve the "Triple Crown of Acting" (Oscar, Emmy, Tony) and an EGOT winner. She commands the screen with profound, emotional performances in projects like *The Woman King* and *How to Get Away with Murder*.
*   [**Angela Bassett**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angela_Bassett): Renowned for intense, biographical roles such as Tina Turner in *What's Love Got to Do with It* and Queen Ramonda in *Black Panther*. She received an Academy Honorary Award for her legendary contributions to film.
*   [**Damson Idris**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Damson_Idris): Gained immense critical acclaim for his lead role as Franklin Saint in the FX series *Snowfall*. He is recognized as one of the most promising dramatic actors of his generation.
*   [**Tessa Thompson**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tessa_Thompson): A highly versatile actor anchoring major franchises like the MCU (*Thor: Ragnarok*) and *Creed*. She is also heavily involved in producing indie films that highlight marginalized voices.
*   [**Mahershala Ali**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mahershala_Ali): A two-time Academy Award winner for *Moonlight* and *Green Book*. He is highly respected for his intellectual, precise acting style and became the first Muslim actor to win an Oscar.
*   [**Colman Domingo**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colman_Domingo): An Emmy-winning actor, playwright, and director whose career surged with roles in *Euphoria* and *Rustin*. He is celebrated as a commanding, sharply dressed leading man in modern cinema.
*   [**Issa Rae**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Issa_Rae): Creator and star of the groundbreaking HBO series *Insecure*. She is an influential writer and producer who reshaped modern TV by authenticating the everyday Black experience.
*   [**Tyler Perry**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tyler_Perry): A pioneering media mogul, actor, and billionaire who built an empire outside the traditional Hollywood system. He owns one of the largest film production studios in the United States, based in Atlanta.
*   [**Donald Glover**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donald_Glover): A true polymath known for his surreal, Emmy-winning show *Atlanta* and his Grammy-winning music under the alter-ego Childish Gambino. He excels as an actor, writer, director, and musician.
*   [**Quinta Brunson**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quinta_Brunson): Created and starred in the phenomenally successful sitcom *Abbott Elementary*. Her work single-handedly revitalized network comedy while highlighting the struggles of public school teachers.
*   [**Keke Palmer**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Keke_Palmer): An Emmy-winning multi-hyphenate known for her charisma as a child star (*Akeelah and the Bee*) and her adult roles (*Nope*). She is also an entrepreneur, singer, and digital media trailblazer.
*   [**Sidney Poitier**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sidney_Poitier): The first Black man to win the Academy Award for Best Actor. He broke Hollywood color barriers with dignified, leading performances in films like *Lilies of the Field* and *Guess Who’s Coming to Dinner*.
*   [**Chadwick Boseman**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chadwick_Boseman): Beloved for portraying icons like Jackie Robinson and James Brown, and for redefining superhero cinema as T’Challa in *Black Panther*. His legacy includes elevating Black stories with depth and pride.
*   [**Halle Berry**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Halle_Berry): The first Black woman to win the Academy Award for Best Actress. She’s known for a wide range of roles from prestige dramas to action franchises.
*   [**Lupita Nyong'o**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lupita_Nyong%27o): An Academy Award–winning actor acclaimed for *12 Years a Slave*, *Us*, and *Black Panther*. She’s recognized for championing representation and literary adaptation.

## 🎤 The Music & Media Titans
*   [**Beyoncé**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9): Holding the record for the most Grammy wins of all time. She continuously redefines visual art, live performances, and genre fusion, solidifying herself as one of history's greatest entertainers.
*   [**Rihanna**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rihanna): Fused her massive pop and R&B music footprint into a billionaire corporate empire. Through Fenty Beauty and Savage X Fenty, she revolutionized shade inclusivity in the beauty industry.
*   [**Kendrick Lamar**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kendrick_Lamar): Often hailed as the greatest rapper of his generation. He is the first hip-hop artist to win a Pulitzer Prize for Music, celebrated for his dense storytelling and cultural commentary.
*   [**Usher**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Usher_%28musician%29): The defining R&B artist of the 2000s, known for his elite vocal ability and unmatched dancing. He remains a top global performer, evidenced by his record-breaking Las Vegas residency and Super Bowl Halftime show.
*   [**Tems**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tems): A Nigerian singer-songwriter who helped propel Afrobeats to massive global pop success. She won a Grammy and received an Oscar nomination for her distinct, soulful voice and global appeal.
*   [**Drake**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drake_%28musician%29): A Canadian rapper and singer whose melodic style redefined modern hip-hop and chart performance. He holds the record for the most Billboard Hot 100 entries in the chart's history.
*   [**SZA**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SZA): Reshaped modern R&B by blending emotionally raw, deeply personal songwriting with genre-defying production. Her album *SOS* broke numerous streaming and chart records for R&B artists.
*   [**Coco Jones**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coco_Jones): A former child star who successfully transitioned into a Grammy-winning R&B sensation. She is widely praised for her rich, traditional R&B vocals and leading presence in Hollywood.
*   [**Samara Joy**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samara_Joy): A generational jazz vocal prodigy who won Grammy Awards for Best New Artist and Best Jazz Vocal Album. She is introducing classic jazz traditions to younger, mainstream audiences.
*   [**Whitney Houston**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Whitney_Houston): One of the best‑selling music artists of all time with an unmatched vocal instrument. Her renditions of “I Will Always Love You” and the national anthem set enduring standards.
*   [**Prince**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prince_(musician)): A singular artist who fused rock, funk, pop, and soul. A prolific songwriter and producer, he challenged industry norms on ownership and creativity.
*   [**Stevie Wonder**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stevie_Wonder): A Motown prodigy turned musical genius. His classic 1970s albums reshaped R&B, pop, and socially conscious songwriting.
*   [**Aretha Franklin**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aretha_Franklin): The “Queen of Soul,” known for timeless vocal power and civil‑rights anthems like “Respect.” A model of artistry and cultural leadership.
*   [**Jay-Z**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jay-Z): Billionaire rapper‑mogul and co‑founder of Roc‑A‑Fella, Roc Nation, and ventures across tech, sports, and luxury. A blueprint for music‑to‑business excellence.

## 💼 Business Leaders & Entrepreneurs
*Focus on wealth creation, historic businesses, and modern moguls.*
*   [**Robert F. Smith**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_F._Smith_%28investor%29): The billionaire founder of Vista Equity Partners and one of the wealthiest Black Americans. He made headlines by wiping out the student debt of Morehouse College’s 2019 graduating class.
*   [**Madam C.J. Walker**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madam_C._J._Walker): Historically recognized as the first female self-made millionaire in America. She created specialized hair products for African American hair and built an enormous beauty empire in the early 1900s.
*   [**Oprah Winfrey**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey): Transformed a daytime talk show into an unparalleled media and cultural empire. She is a billionaire proprietor, philanthropist, and one of the most trusted voices in modern media.
*   [**Daymond John**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daymond_John): Started the iconic urban clothing brand FUBU out of his mother's house and grew it into a global phenomenon. He is currently an influential angel investor on the hit show *Shark Tank*.
*   [**Reginald F. Lewis**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reginald_F._Lewis): The first Black billionaire in the US context. By acquiring Beatrice International Foods, he orchestrated the largest leveraged buyout of overseas assets at the time, leaving a lasting Wall Street legacy.
*   [**Robert L. Johnson**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_L._Johnson): Co‑founder of BET and the first Black American billionaire after selling BET to Viacom. His ventures span media, hotels, and private equity.
*   [**Cathy Hughes**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cathy_Hughes): Founder of Radio One (now Urban One), the largest African‑American‑owned broadcasting company in the U.S., and a pioneer of targeted urban media.
*   [**Mellody Hobson**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mellody_Hobson): Co‑CEO of Ariel Investments and board leader (Starbucks, JPMorgan). A prominent voice on financial literacy and corporate governance.
*   [**Kenneth Chenault**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kenneth_Chenault): Former CEO of American Express, one of the first Black CEOs of a Fortune 500 company. Known for steady leadership through crises.
*   [**Ursula Burns**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ursula_Burns): The first Black woman to serve as CEO of a Fortune 500 company (Xerox). A trailblazer for inclusion in engineering and corporate leadership.
*   [**Byron Allen**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Byron_Allen): Media entrepreneur and owner of Entertainment Studios/Allen Media Group. Built a multi‑billion‑dollar portfolio across TV networks and digital properties.
*   [**Aliko Dangote**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aliko_Dangote): Africa’s richest person, founder of the Dangote Group spanning cement, sugar, and industrials. A case study in scale and infrastructure in emerging markets.

## ⚖️ Government Officials & Civil Rights Leaders
*Focus on history, justice, and the political sphere.*
*   [**Barack Obama**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barack_Obama): The 44th President of the United States. He made history by running a grassroots campaign to become the nation’s first Black president, famously overseeing healthcare reform and the recovery from the 2008 financial crisis.
*   [**Kamala Harris**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kamala_Harris): The 49th Vice President of the United States. She broke historic barriers as the first female, first African American, and first South Asian American Vice President in US history.
*   [**Thurgood Marshall**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thurgood_Marshall): Before becoming the Supreme Court’s first Black Justice, he was a pioneering civil rights lawyer. He successfully argued the landmark *Brown v. Board of Education* case, which desegregated public schools.
*   [**Shirley Chisholm**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shirley_Chisholm): A trailblazer who became the first Black woman elected to the United States Congress. She fearlessly became the first woman and first African American to seek the presidential nomination of a major political party.
*   [**John Lewis**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Lewis_%28civil_rights_leader%29): A giant of the civil rights movement and one of the original Freedom Riders. He suffered severe beatings marching for voting rights in Selma, Alabama, before serving for decades as the "Conscience of the Congress."
*   [**Ketanji Brown Jackson**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ketanji_Brown_Jackson): Made history as the first Black woman to serve as a Justice on the United States Supreme Court. Previously, she served as a federal public defender, bringing vital new perspective to the nation's highest court.
*   [**Martin Luther King Jr.**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Martin_Luther_King_Jr.): Baptist minister and architect of the nonviolent civil‑rights movement. Led the Montgomery Bus Boycott and March on Washington; Nobel Peace Prize laureate.
*   [**Malcolm X**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malcolm_X): Influential human‑rights activist who advocated Black self‑determination and dignity. His life and speeches reshaped civil‑rights discourse.
*   [**Rosa Parks**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rosa_Parks): “Mother of the Civil Rights Movement.” Her refusal to surrender a bus seat in Montgomery sparked a mass boycott and national momentum.
*   [**Harriet Tubman**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harriet_Tubman): Abolitionist and Underground Railroad conductor who led enslaved people to freedom. Later served as a Union spy and advocate for women’s suffrage.
*   [**Frederick Douglass**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frederick_Douglass): Formerly enslaved abolitionist, orator, and author. His writings and speeches powerfully advanced the cause of emancipation and equal rights.
*   [**Ida B. Wells**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ida_B._Wells): Journalist and anti‑lynching crusader. Co‑founder of the NAACP and a pioneering data‑driven investigator of racial violence.

## 🔭 Science, Technology & Literature
*Focus on pioneers in STEM and thought leadership.*
*   [**Mae Jemison**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mae_Jemison): An engineer, physician, and former NASA astronaut. In 1992, aboard the Space Shuttle Endeavour, she made history as the first Black woman to travel in space.
*   [**Neil deGrasse Tyson**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neil_deGrasse_Tyson): A world-renowned astrophysicist and head of the Hayden Planetarium. He is highly praised for his skill as a science communicator, making deeply complex cosmic concepts accessible to the general public.
*   [**Katherine Johnson**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Katherine_Johnson): A legendary NASA mathematician whose brilliant orbital mechanic calculations were critical to the success of the first and subsequent US crewed spaceflights. Her story inspired the film *Hidden Figures*.
*   [**Toni Morrison**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toni_Morrison): A foundational figure of American literature. The renowned author of *Beloved* and *Song of Solomon*, she was the first African American woman to win the Nobel Prize in Literature.
*   [**James Baldwin**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Baldwin): A brilliant novelist, playwright, and essayist. His impassioned writings explored racial, sexual, and class distinctions in Western societies, making him a critical intellectual voice of the mid-20th century civil rights movement.
*   [**George Washington Carver**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Washington_Carver): Agricultural scientist and inventor. Advanced crop rotation and peanut‑based products that transformed Southern farming and soil health.
*   [**Charles R. Drew**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_R._Drew): Surgeon and medical researcher who pioneered large‑scale blood banking and plasma preservation, saving countless lives.
*   [**Gladys West**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gladys_West): Mathematician whose geodesy work underpins GPS accuracy worldwide. An unsung architect of modern navigation.
*   [**Lonnie Johnson**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lonnie_Johnson_(inventor)): NASA engineer and prolific inventor best known for creating the Super Soaker; holder of over 100 patents.
*   [**Guion Bluford**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guion_Bluford): Engineer and Air Force pilot who became the first Black American in space (1983), expanding representation in aerospace.

## 🏅 Sports Icons & Athletes
*Focus on legendary athletes who broke barriers and dominated globally.*
*   [**Muhammad Ali**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad_Ali): Remembered as the "Greatest of All Time" in heavyweight boxing. Outside the ring, he was a defining global icon of civil rights, resisting the Vietnam War draft based on his deeply held religious convictions.
*   [**Serena Williams**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serena_Williams): Won an astounding 23 Grand Slam women's singles titles. Her overpowering athleticism and longevity fundamentally changed the sport of tennis while overcoming intense racial prejudice.
*   [**Michael Jordan**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Jordan): A six-time NBA champion who popularized basketball around the world in the 1980s and 1990s. His cultural impact birthed the modern sneaker industry through the incredibly successful Jordan Brand.
*   [**LeBron James**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LeBron_James): A four-time NBA champion and the NBA's all-time leading scorer. Known for unprecedented athletic longevity, he is also a successful media mogul and philanthropist who funded the "I Promise School".
*   [**Simone Biles**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simone_Biles): Commonly cited as the greatest gymnast of all time, with 30 World Championship medals. She possesses peerless physical dominance and sparked crucial conversations surrounding athletes' mental health.
*   [**Jackie Robinson**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jackie_Robinson): Courageously broke the baseball color line when he started at first base for the Brooklyn Dodgers in 1947. He faced incredible abuse strictly with stellar play, ending racial segregation in professional baseball.
*   [**Arthur Ashe**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arthur_Ashe): The first Black player selected to the US Davis Cup team and the only Black man ever to win the singles title at Wimbledon, the US Open, and the Australian Open. Unafraid to use his platform, he championed civil rights and AIDS awareness.
*   [**Jesse Owens**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jesse_Owens): Achieved international fame at the 1936 Summer Olympics in Berlin, Germany. By winning four gold medals in track and field, he directly dispelled Adolf Hitler's myth of Aryan supremacy on the world stage.
*   [**Tiger Woods**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tiger_Woods): One of golf’s greatest champions. His global impact diversified the sport and set modern records for wins and popularity.
*   [**Kobe Bryant**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kobe_Bryant): Five‑time NBA champion and 18‑time All‑Star. Revered for “Mamba Mentality,” he also won an Oscar for his short film *Dear Basketball*.
*   [**Venus Williams**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Venus_Williams): Multiple‑time Grand Slam champion who transformed women’s tennis with power and advocacy, including the fight for equal prize money at Wimbledon.
*   [**Jackie Joyner-Kersee**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jackie_Joyner-Kersee): Track and field legend; world‑record holder in the heptathlon. A model of longevity, versatility, and community leadership.
