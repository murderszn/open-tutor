# 🧪 Reproduction — Quick Guide

Asexual reproduction: One parent makes offspring without joining egg and sperm.
- Examples: budding (yeast/hydra), binary fission (bacteria), runners (strawberries).
- Result: Offspring are genetically very similar to the parent.

Sexual reproduction: Two parents contribute egg and sperm.
- Result: Offspring combine DNA from both, increasing variation.

Cell division basics (7th grade):
- Mitosis: One division → two identical cells; used for growth and repair.
- Meiosis: Two divisions → four cells with half the chromosomes; used to make egg/sperm.

Why variation matters: Helps populations adapt to changes in environment and resist disease.
