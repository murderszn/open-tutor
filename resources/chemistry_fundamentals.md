## Vocabulary (kid‑friendly)
- [Atom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atom): smallest unit of an element that keeps its identity.
- [Element](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_element): pure substance made of one kind of atom (H, O, C).
- [Molecule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecule): two or more atoms bonded together (O₂, H₂O).
- [Compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_compound): substance made of two or more elements chemically bonded (H₂O, CO₂).
- [Mixture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mixture): two or more substances physically combined (salt water, air).
- Particles — [Proton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proton) / [Neutron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neutron) / [Electron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electron): positive / neutral / negative; electrons occupy regions around the nucleus.
- [Nucleus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atomic_nucleus): center of the atom (protons + neutrons), very small and dense.
- [Isotope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isotope): same element, different number of neutrons (C‑12 vs C‑14).
- [Ion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ion): charged atom or molecule (lost/gained electrons); [Cation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cation) (+), [Anion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anion) (–).
- [Valence electron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Valence_electron): outer‑shell electron(s) that take part in bonding; helps predict reactivity.
- [Periodic table](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Periodic_table): organized chart of elements; groups (columns) share properties, periods (rows) share energy levels.
- Classes — [Metals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metal) / [Nonmetals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nonmetal) / [Metalloids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metalloid): broad groups with typical properties (conductivity, luster, etc.).
- [Ionic bond](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ionic_bonding): transfer of electrons (metal + nonmetal); forms ions that attract (NaCl).
- [Covalent bond](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Covalent_bond): sharing electrons (nonmetal + nonmetal) (H₂O, CO₂).
- [Chemical reaction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_reaction): atoms rearrange bonds; reactants → products; mass is conserved.
- Changes — [Physical change](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physical_change) vs [Chemical change](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_change): change in form/state vs making new substances.
- [Stoichiometric coefficient](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stoichiometry): the big number before a formula when balancing equations (e.g., 2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O).
- [Solution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solution) / [Solute](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solute) / [Solvent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solvent): a uniform mixture; solute dissolves in solvent (salt in water).

## Facts & Numbers (handy)
- Proton charge: +1; Neutron: 0; Electron: −1
- Relative masses: proton ≈ 1 u; neutron ≈ 1 u; electron ≈ 1/1836 u (u = atomic mass unit)
- Hydrogen (H) atomic number 1; Carbon (C) 6; Oxygen (O) 8; Sodium (Na) 11; Chlorine (Cl) 17
- Common diatomics: H₂, N₂, O₂, F₂, Cl₂, Br₂, I₂
- Water: H₂O (≈ 11% H, 89% O by mass); density ≈ 1 g/mL at room temp
- Table salt: NaCl (ionic); Carbon dioxide: CO₂ (covalent)
- pH scale: 7 = neutral (pure water), <7 acid, >7 base (simple classroom scale)
- Law of conservation of mass: total mass of reactants = total mass of products
- Avogadro’s number (curiosity): 6.022 × 10²³ particles per mole

## Equations & Formats (textbook layout)

Balanced reaction (conservation of mass)

$$
2\,\mathrm{H_2} + \mathrm{O_2} \;\longrightarrow\; 2\,\mathrm{H_2O}
$$

Ionic compounds (charge balance)

$$
\mathrm{Na}^{+} + \mathrm{Cl}^{-} \;\longrightarrow\; \mathrm{NaCl} \\
\mathrm{Mg}^{2+} + 2\,\mathrm{Cl}^{-} \;\longrightarrow\; \mathrm{MgCl_2}
$$

Common covalent formulas (molecular)

$$
\mathrm{CO_2},\quad \mathrm{H_2O},\quad \mathrm{NH_3},\quad \mathrm{CH_4}
$$

Density (often used in labs)

$$
\rho = \frac{m}{V} \quad \big( \rho:\ \mathrm{g\!/cm^3\ or\ kg\!/m^3} \big)
$$
