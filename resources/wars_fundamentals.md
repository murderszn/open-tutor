# 🌍 Global Conflicts & State-Building — A Quick Guide

This guide highlights major wars and conflicts that redirected world leadership, redrew borders, or created new institutions and governments. It’s a study companion for understanding why certain events loom large in history.

How to read wars
- Causes: resources, ideology, power balance, alliances, colonialism.
- Outcomes: borders, new states, treaties, institutions, economic orders.
- Signals: which powers rose or declined; how citizens’ lives changed.

## Early Turning Points

- Thirty Years’ War (1618–1648) — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thirty_Years%27_War)
  - Why it matters: Ends with the Treaties of Westphalia → modern idea of state sovereignty and non‑interference.
- Napoleonic Wars (1803–1815) — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Napoleonic_Wars)
  - Why it matters: Congress of Vienna redraws Europe; “balance of power” diplomacy to prevent another Napoleon.
- American Revolutionary War (1775–1783) — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War)
  - Why it matters: Creates the United States; inspires constitutional government and later independence movements.
- French Revolutionary & Napoleonic Era (1792–1815) — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Revolutionary_Wars)
  - Why it matters: Ends absolute monarchy in France; spreads legal and civic reforms that echo across Europe.

## World Wars (20th Century)

- World War I (1914–1918) — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_I)
  - Outcomes: End of empires (Ottoman, Austro‑Hungarian, Russian, German). League of Nations founded. New borders in Europe and the Middle East.
  - Direction of leadership: US arrives as a creditor power; fragile order set by Versailles.
- Russian Civil War (1917–1923) — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Civil_War)
  - Outcomes: Formation of the Soviet Union (USSR), one pole of 20th‑century power.
- Chinese Civil War (1927–1949) — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Civil_War)
  - Outcomes: People’s Republic of China on the mainland; Republic of China in Taiwan.
- World War II (1939–1945) — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_II)
  - Outcomes: United Nations created; Bretton Woods institutions (IMF, World Bank); start of US‑Soviet Cold War; decolonization accelerates; Israel’s path to statehood (UN Partition 1947; 1948 war).
  - Direction of leadership: US and USSR emerge as superpowers; Europe rebuilt under new security and economic frameworks.

## Cold War Conflicts & State Formation

- Korean War (1950–1953) — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_War)
  - Outcomes: Two Koreas (DMZ). US‑led alliances in Asia; test of containment policy.
- Vietnam War (1955–1975) — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnam_War)
  - Outcomes: Vietnam reunified under communist government; deep impact on US policy and global movements.
- Algerian War of Independence (1954–1962) — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algerian_War)
  - Outcomes: Independence from France; symbol of broader African/Asian decolonization.
- Arab–Israeli Wars (1948, 1956, 1967, 1973) — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab%E2%80%93Israeli_conflict)
  - Outcomes: Israel’s survival and changing borders; later peace with Egypt (1979) and Jordan (1994); long‑term questions on Palestinians and regional order.
- Indo‑Pakistani Wars & Bangladesh (1947–1971) — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indo-Pakistani_wars_and_conflicts)
  - Outcomes: Partition produces India & Pakistan; 1971 war creates Bangladesh.
- Iran–Iraq War (1980–1988) — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iran%E2%80%93Iraq_War)
  - Outcomes: Massive casualties; entrenched rivalries; reshaped Gulf security thinking.
- Yugoslav Wars (1991–2001) — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yugoslav_Wars)
  - Outcomes: Breakup into successor states; NATO interventions; war‑crimes tribunals strengthen international justice norms.

## Post‑1990 Order & Shocks

- Gulf War (1990–1991) — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_War)
  - Outcomes: US‑led coalition reverses Iraq’s invasion of Kuwait; signals new collective security.
- Iraq War (2003–2011) — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iraq_War)
  - Outcomes: Regime change; long insurgency; regional realignments; debate on intervention and international law.
- Syrian Civil War (2011– ) — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syrian_civil_war)
  - Outcomes: Humanitarian catastrophe; rise/fall of ISIS; Russian, Iranian, Turkish, US roles; refugee flows reshape politics.
- Russia–Ukraine Conflict (2014– ; full‑scale invasion 2022) — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russo-Ukrainian_War)
  - Outcomes: Sanctions, NATO posture shifts, EU security debates. Verify current lines of control in reliable news sources.

## Contemporary Middle East Flashpoints (Context)

- Iran–Israel Tensions (long‑running) — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iran%E2%80%93Israel_proxy_conflict)
  - Nature: A “shadow war” involving proxies, cyberattacks, covert strikes, and regional militias.
  - US policy context (2010s): Iran nuclear deal (JCPOA, 2015); US withdrawal (2018); strike on Qasem Soleimani (2020); normalization via Abraham Accords (2020). Events evolve rapidly—verify current status via reliable, up‑to‑date sources.

Note on current events: Details in the 2020s change quickly. Always check multiple reputable outlets before citing specifics.

## Institutions Created or Reinforced by War

- State sovereignty: Treaties of Westphalia (1648)
- Balance of power diplomacy: Congress of Vienna (1815)
- International cooperation: League of Nations (1919, dissolved), United Nations (1945– )
- Economic order: Bretton Woods system (IMF, World Bank) (1944– )
- Collective security: NATO (1949– ), Warsaw Pact (1955–1991)
- Regional integration (postwar): European Coal & Steel Community → European Union (1951 →)
