## Vocabulary (kid‑friendly)
- [Unit of measurement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unit_of_measurement) (symbol): agreed way to measure a quantity; e.g., meter (m), second (s), kilogram (kg). See also [SI units](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_System_of_Units).
- [Distance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distance) vs [Displacement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Displacement_(vector)): how far you travel along a path vs straight‑line change in position with direction (vector).
- [Speed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Speed) vs [Velocity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Velocity): how fast you move (no direction) vs how fast and in what direction (vector).
- [Acceleration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acceleration): how quickly velocity changes (speeding up, slowing down, or turning), usually in m/s².
- [Force](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Force) (F): a push or pull; the [net force](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Resultant_force) is the total after adding forces with directions.
- [Mass](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mass) vs [Weight](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weight): amount of matter (kg) vs the pull of gravity on that mass (newtons, N).
- [Inertia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inertia): tendency of an object to resist changes in its motion (linked to mass).
- [Friction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Friction): a force that resists sliding or rolling when surfaces touch.
- [Work](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Work_(physics)) (W): force acting through a distance; moving something in the direction of the force.
- [Energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Energy): ability to do work; includes [kinetic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kinetic_energy) (motion) and [potential](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Potential_energy) (stored) forms.
- [Temperature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temperature) vs [Heat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heat): how hot (average particle energy) vs energy that flows because of a temperature difference.
- Heat transfer — [Conduction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermal_conduction) / [Convection](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Convection) / [Radiation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermal_radiation): by touching / moving fluid / electromagnetic waves.
- Wave terms: [Wavelength](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wavelength) (λ), [Frequency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frequency) (f), [Amplitude](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amplitude) (height), [Wave speed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wave_velocity) (v).
- [Reflection](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reflection_(physics)) / [Refraction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Refraction): bouncing vs bending of waves (including light and sound).

## Facts & Numbers (handy)
- Gravity near Earth’s surface: g ≈ 9.8 m/s² (use 10 m/s² for quick math)
- Speed of light in vacuum: c ≈ 3.00 × 10⁸ m/s
- Sound in air (room temp): ≈ 340 m/s
- Density of liquid water: 1.0 g/cm³ = 1000 kg/m³
- Room temperature: 20–22 °C (68–72 °F)
- Absolute zero: −273.15 °C = 0 K
- Metric prefixes: kilo 10³, centi 10⁻², milli 10⁻³

## Formulas (textbook layout)

Speed / velocity

$$
v = \frac{d}{t} \quad [\mathrm{m\!/s}]
$$

Acceleration

$$
a = \frac{\Delta v}{\Delta t} \quad [\mathrm{m\!/s^2}]
$$

Newton’s second law

$$
F = m\,a \quad [\mathrm{N}]
$$

Weight (near Earth’s surface)

$$
W = m\,g \quad [\mathrm{N}],\quad g \approx 9.8\,\mathrm{m\!/s^2}
$$

Kinetic energy

$$
E_{k} = \tfrac{1}{2}\,m\,v^{2} \quad [\mathrm{J}]
$$

Gravitational potential energy

$$
E_{p} = m\,g\,h \quad [\mathrm{J}]
$$

Heat / temperature change

$$
Q = m\,c\,\Delta T \quad [\mathrm{J}]
$$

Wave relation

$$
v = f\,\lambda \quad \big( v:\ \mathrm{m\!/s},\ f:\ \mathrm{Hz},\ \lambda:\ \mathrm{m} \big)
$$
