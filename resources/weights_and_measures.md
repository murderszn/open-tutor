## рЯ•Ы Volume & Liquid Measurements (US Customary)

When you're cooking or measuring liquids in the US, you'll use these units. 

**The "Gallon Man" Breakdown:**
*   **1 Gallon (gal)** = 4 Quarts = 8 Pints = 16 Cups = 128 fluid ounces

**Small Kitchen Measurements:**
*   **1 Tablespoon (tbsp)** = 3 Teaspoons (tsp)
*   **1 fluid ounce (fl oz)** = 2 Tablespoons

> рЯТ° **Tip:** A normal can of soda is **12 fluid ounces** (1.5 cups). A standard bottle of water is usually **16.9 fluid ounces** (just over 2 cups).

## рЯПЛпЄП Weight & Mass (US Customary)

How heavy is it? In the US, we use ounces, pounds, and tons.

*   **1 Pound (lb)** = 16 Ounces (oz)
*   **1 Ton (T)** = 2,000 Pounds

> рЯТ° **Tip:** A slice of bread weighs about **1 ounce**. A standard football weighs about **1 pound**. A small car weighs about **1 ton**.

## рЯУП Length & Distance (US Customary)

How long or how far away is it?

*   **1 Foot (ft)** = 12 Inches (in)
*   **1 Yard (yd)** = 3 Feet = 36 Inches
*   **1 Mile (mi)** = 5,280 Feet = 1,760 Yards

> рЯТ° **Tip:** An adult's foot is (roughly) **1 foot** long. A standard door is about **7 feet** tall. Running 4 laps around a high school track is exactly **1 mile**.

## рЯМН The Metric System (International Standard)

Most of the world (and all of science!) uses the Metric System. It is based on the number **10**, which makes it incredibly easy to scale up and down just by moving the decimal point!

### Metric Prefixes
*   **Kilo-** = 1,000
*   **Hecto-** = 100
*   **Deca-** = 10
*   *(Base Unit)* = 1
*   **Deci-** = 0.1 (1/10th)
*   **Centi-** = 0.01 (1/100th)
*   **Milli-** = 0.001 (1/1000th)

### Length (Base Unit: Meter)
*   **1 Kilometer (km)** = 1,000 Meters
*   **1 Meter (m)** = 100 Centimeters = 1,000 Millimeters
*   **1 Centimeter (cm)** = 10 Millimeters

### Mass / Weight (Base Unit: Gram)
*   **1 Kilogram (kg)** = 1,000 Grams
*   **1 Gram (g)** = 1,000 Milligrams (mg)

### Volume / Liquid (Base Unit: Liter)
*   **1 Liter (L)** = 1,000 Milliliters (mL)

## рЯМ°пЄП Temperature

*   **Fahrenheit ($^\circ\text{F}$)** $\rightarrow$ **Celsius ($^\circ\text{C}$):** Subtract 32, then multiply by $5/9$.
    *   *Formula: $C = (F - 32) \times 5/9$*
*   **Celsius ($^\circ\text{C}$)** $\rightarrow$ **Fahrenheit ($^\circ\text{F}$):** Multiply by $9/5$, then add 32.
    *   *Formula: $F = (C \times 9/5) + 32$*

**Important Milestones:**
*   **Water Freezes:** $32^\circ\text{F}$ | $0^\circ\text{C}$
*   **Normal Room Temp:** $68^\circ\text{F}$ to $72^\circ\text{F}$ | $20^\circ\text{C}$ to $22^\circ\text{C}$
*   **Normal Body Temp:** $98.6^\circ\text{F}$ | $37^\circ\text{C}$
*   **Water Boils:** $212^\circ\text{F}$ | $100^\circ\text{C}$

## рЯТЊ Digital Storage Sizes

How big is a file, a photo, or a hard drive? Computers store data in bytes.

*   **1 Bit (b)** = A single binary digit (0 or 1)
*   **1 Byte (B)** = 8 Bits (Enough to store one letter or character of text)
*   **1 Kilobyte (KB)** $\approx$ 1,000 Bytes (A short paragraph of simple text)
*   **1 Megabyte (MB)** $\approx$ 1,000 Kilobytes (A typical smartphone photo or a minute of MP3 audio)
*   **1 Gigabyte (GB)** $\approx$ 1,000 Megabytes (A 30-minute HD TV show or a short movie)
*   **1 Terabyte (TB)** $\approx$ 1,000 Gigabytes (A large modern computer hard drive)
*   **1 Petabyte (PB)** $\approx$ 1,000 Terabytes (1.5 million CD-ROMs worth of data)
*   **1 Exabyte (EB)** $\approx$ 1,000 Petabytes (All words ever spoken by human beings would fit in roughly 5 Exabytes!)

*(Note: In traditional computer science memory, multipliers are mathematically based on 1024 (e.g., 1 KB = 1024 Bytes), but modern storage manufacturers mostly use the standard 1,000 metric base for hard drives.)*
