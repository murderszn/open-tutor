## 🗂️ Vocabulary (kid‑friendly)
- Cell: the basic unit of life; smallest unit that can live and reproduce.
- Organelle: tiny parts inside cells that do jobs (e.g., nucleus, mitochondria, chloroplasts).
- Nucleus: control center of eukaryotic cells that holds DNA.
- Mitochondria: “powerhouses” that release energy from food.
- Chloroplasts: green parts in plant cells that capture sunlight for photosynthesis.
- Photosynthesis: using sunlight to make sugar from water and carbon dioxide.
- Cellular respiration: releasing energy from sugar using oxygen.
- Mitosis vs meiosis: cell division for growth/repair vs making egg/sperm with half the DNA.
- Homeostasis: keeping body/cell conditions steady (like temperature, water, pH).
- Ecosystem: living things and nonliving environment working together.
## 💧 Water Cycle

The continuous movement of water on, above, and below Earth’s surface driven by the Sun.

- Stages: [Evaporation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evaporation) → [Condensation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Condensation) → [Precipitation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Precipitation) → Collection
- Plus: [Transpiration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transpiration), [Runoff](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Surface_runoff), Infiltration/Groundwater
- Why it matters: Weather patterns, fresh water supply, soil and ecosystems

Diagram (typical order):
```
Sun heats water  →  Evaporation ↑
                     ↓ (air cools)
                 Condensation (clouds)
                     ↓
                 Precipitation (rain/snow)
                     ↓
  Collection (lakes/rivers/ocean); runoff & infiltration
```

Local examples: puddles drying (evaporation), dew on grass (condensation), rainstorm (precipitation), rivers/lakes (collection).

## 🌿 Plant Cells

Plant cells are eukaryotic cells with structures specialized for support and photosynthesis.

- Key organelles: [Cell wall](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cell_wall), [Cell membrane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cell_membrane), [Nucleus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cell_nucleus), [Cytoplasm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cytoplasm), [Chloroplasts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chloroplast), [Vacuole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vacuole), [Mitochondria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mitochondrion)
- Also important: [Ribosome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ribosome), [Endoplasmic reticulum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Endoplasmic_reticulum), [Golgi apparatus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Golgi_apparatus)
- Quick contrast: Plants have a cell wall and chloroplasts; animals do not.

Simple diagram (not to scale):
```
[Cell wall]
|  membrane  |
|  nucleus   |  chloroplast  chloroplast
|  vacuole   |  mitochondrion  cytoplasm
| ribosomes, ER, Golgi ... |
```

## 🐾 Animal Cells

Animal cells are eukaryotic cells without a cell wall or chloroplasts.

- Key organelles: [Cell membrane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cell_membrane), [Nucleus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cell_nucleus), [Cytoplasm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cytoplasm), [Mitochondria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mitochondrion)
- Also common: [Ribosome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ribosome), [Endoplasmic reticulum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Endoplasmic_reticulum), [Golgi apparatus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Golgi_apparatus), [Lysosome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lysosome)
- Quick contrast: No cell wall or chloroplasts; shapes are often rounder/irregular.

Simple diagram (not to scale):
```
( cell membrane )
(  nucleus      )  mitochondria    ribosomes/ER/Golgi in cytoplasm
(  cytoplasm    )
```

## 🔬 Cell Theory & Cell Types (Quick)

- [Cell theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cell_theory):
  - All living things are made of one or more cells.
  - The cell is the basic unit of structure and function in life.
  - All cells come from pre‑existing cells.
- Cell types:
  - [Prokaryotes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prokaryote): no nucleus (bacteria). Simple, single‑celled.
  - [Eukaryotes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryote): nucleus present (plants, animals, fungi, protists).

## ☀️ Photosynthesis

How plants use sunlight to make food (sugar) and release oxygen.

- In words: Plants use sunlight to combine water (H₂O) and carbon dioxide (CO₂) to make sugar (glucose) and oxygen (O₂).
- Equation (balanced): 6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂
- Where it happens: [Chloroplasts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chloroplast) with [Chlorophyll](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chlorophyll)
- Connections: [Photosynthesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photosynthesis) feeds food webs; oxygen supports life; links to [Cellular respiration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cellular_respiration).

Inputs → outputs diagram:
```
Sunlight + CO₂ + H₂O  →  Glucose (sugar) + O₂
```

## 🌱 Ecosystems & Food Chains (Quick)

- Roles:
  - [Producers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Primary_production): plants/algae make food via photosynthesis.
  - [Consumers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Consumer_(ecology)): animals eat plants/other animals for energy.
  - [Decomposers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decomposer): fungi/bacteria break down dead material, recycle nutrients.
- Flow of energy: Sun → producer → consumer → decomposer.
- Habitats & ecosystems: living (biotic) + nonliving (abiotic: light, water, soil) parts interacting.

Simple chain:
```
Sun → grass (producer) → rabbit (consumer) → fox (consumer) → fungi (decomposer)
```

## 🌍 Carbon Cycle (Very Brief)

- Carbon moves between air, living things, soil, and water.
- Photosynthesis pulls CO₂ from air into plants. Respiration and decomposition return CO₂ to air.
- Human activities (burning fuels, deforestation) change carbon movement.

## 🧪 Reproduction

How living things make more life, and how cells divide.

- Asexual reproduction: one parent, offspring are very similar
  - Examples: [Budding](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Budding), [Binary fission](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Binary_fission), [Runners](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stolon)
- Sexual reproduction: two parents (egg + sperm), more genetic variation
  - Why variation matters: helps populations adapt and resist disease ([Genetic variation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genetic_variation))
- Cell division basics:
  - [Mitosis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mitosis): one division → two identical cells (growth/repair)
  - [Meiosis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meiosis): two divisions → four cells with half the chromosomes (egg/sperm)

Quick compare:
```
Asexual: fast, one parent, low variation
Sexual: slower, two parents, high variation
Mitosis: identical copies for growth/repair
Meiosis: halves chromosomes for gametes
```
